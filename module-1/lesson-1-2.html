The One-Person Startup Era Has Officially Begun

Sam Altman made a bet in a group chat with tech CEOs: the first one-person billion-dollar company is coming, and it's only possible because of AI. In 2024, that sounded absurd. Today, it's already happening — a 50-person company hit $100M in revenue and a $2.1B valuation. A solo founder crossed $1M in year one with no team.

The constraint that made small-team companies impossible at real scale is shrinking fast.

Key Takeaways
Revenue per employee = $2M at Gamma ($100M revenue, ~50 people, $2.1B valuation). The math has changed.
The logic isn't about being solo forever — it's about hiring what AI can't do.
Claude Code changed who can build: non-technical founders can now direct product without waiting on developers.
AI still can't replace strategic judgment, high-trust relationships, and knowing when to pivot.
