The Challenge
Real-World Scenario

Before you touch a single tool, you need to prove to yourself that you already understand something real about this space. Module 1 was about the macro shift — why this moment matters, what kind of thinking wins, and what you're actually building toward.

This challenge has nothing to do with tools. It has everything to do with thinking.

You've just been introduced to a potential client — a local law firm with 8 attorneys. They've heard about AI but have done nothing with it. Before your second conversation, they send you a short message:

"We're open to exploring AI, but honestly we don't know where to start or if it's even relevant for us. Can you send something over?"
Your Challenge
  1. A
    Write the wrong response. Write the reply you would have sent before Module 1 — the one that's vague, or generic, or sounds like a consultant trying to impress someone. Get it out of your system. Write it out completely before moving on.
  2. B
    Write the right response. Write the reply you'd send now. Three to five sentences. It should:
    • Give them one concrete, credible example of how a firm their size has already used AI to save meaningful time or reduce cost
    • Position this as the beginning of a conversation, not a pitch
    • Sound like a person wrote it, not a deck
  3. 3
    Write one sentence underneath Response B that names the biggest difference between the two. Not which one is better — you know that. What specifically changed in how you thought about it.
Your Deliverable
Two responses written. One sentence of reflection. If you skipped Response A, go back — the contrast is the lesson. Keep both somewhere you can find them.
Why this matters: At the end of Module 6 you'll look at these again. The gap between Response A and what you'll be capable of by then is the entire point of this course.
