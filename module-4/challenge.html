The Challenge
Real-World Scenario

Module 4 drew a clear line between automations and agents. You know what that line is now. This challenge asks you to build an end-to-end professional services workflow — from brief to research to client-ready document — and automate at least one step in the chain so it runs without you. You're building for a fictional client. The output should look real enough to send to an actual one.

Step 1 — Generate your environment

Open Claude and ask it to generate a complete working environment for a fictional boutique consulting firm called ClearView Advisory. Ask Claude to produce three clearly labeled documents: a one-page company brief (their services, typical client, voice and tone, and proposal structure — executive summary, research findings, recommended approach, next steps), a client engagement brief for a fictional healthcare staffing client who wants to understand how AI could reduce their administrative burden, and one sample research summary on the healthcare staffing industry. Save all three. The company brief becomes your Cowork Skill. The client brief is your task input. The sample research summary is your quality bar — every output you produce should clear this standard.

Your Challenge
  1. 1
    Build your Skill. Create a Cowork Skill called "ClearView Advisory." Build it from the company brief: who they are, how they communicate, what their proposal structure looks like. Paste the sample summary in as a reference. Test in isolation first: ask Cowork to produce a one-paragraph executive summary for the healthcare staffing client using only the Skill and client brief as input. Refine until it sounds like ClearView, not Claude.
  2. 2
    The Research Brief. Inside a Claude Project with the ClearView Skill and client brief in the knowledge base, use Claude's deep research mode to produce a structured research brief on the healthcare staffing industry. It should directly answer: what are the biggest administrative pain points, where is AI currently being applied in this space, and what are the three highest-ROI opportunities for a firm their size. This document goes directly into your proposal — it should not need editing to be usable.
  3. 3
    The Proposal Document. Using Claude in PowerPoint or Word (as shown in Lesson 33), build a client-ready proposal applying the ClearView structure: executive summary, research findings, recommended approach, next steps. If Claude in PowerPoint isn't available, build it as a well-formatted Google Doc or Claude Artifact. The standard: it should look like something you would actually send to a client.
  4. 4
    Automate one step. Set up a Cowork task or Zapier workflow that automates one repeating step in this pipeline.
    • Option A: when you add a new client brief to a Google Sheet row, trigger a Cowork task that drafts the executive summary section automatically.
    • Option B: when the proposal is complete, trigger a task that drafts a follow-up email pulling the client's name, company, and engagement topic from the brief — and saves it as a draft in your own Gmail.
    Either option should run with one trigger. Test it. The test run is part of the deliverable.
Your Deliverable
  • A ClearView Cowork Skill that produces on-brand output
  • A research brief from deep research mode that's usable without editing
  • A proposal document that passes the "would I actually send this?" test
  • One automated step that runs with a single trigger and produces output in your own tools
Extension
Set up a complete Cowork task that takes a new client brief as input and produces all four proposal sections in sequence — executive summary, research, recommendations, next steps — using ClearView's voice throughout. Run it against the healthcare staffing brief. Time how long it takes from trigger to finished document.
