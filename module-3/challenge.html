The Challenge
Real-World Scenario

Module 3 moved you from prompting to delegating. You've configured Cowork, wired connectors, set up Zapier MCP, and watched what a real automation pipeline looks like running. Now you prove you can build one.

This challenge is structured deliberately. You're not sending emails to real people. You're not wiring to a live business. You're building and testing a complete automation system using yourself as the only recipient — because that's how every professional builds automations before they hand them to a client. You test it on yourself first.

Step 1 — Generate your environment

Open Claude and ask it to generate the following for a fictional home services company called Ridgeline Home Services: a company brief (what they do — HVAC, landscaping, cleaning — their communication style, their average client, and what makes a good follow-up message from them), a lead tracking sheet with 10 fictional leads (names, service requested, date of inquiry, follow-up status — a mix of fresh leads from the last 24 hours and overdue ones with no contact in 3–5 days), and one sample follow-up message for an overdue lead written in Ridgeline's voice.

Copy the lead data into a Google Sheet with these columns: Name, Service, Inquiry Date, Follow-Up Status, Draft Message. Leave the Draft Message column empty — that's what your automation will fill. Save the company brief. This becomes your Skill.

Your Challenge
  1. 2
    Build your Skill. Open Cowork. Create a new Skill called Ridgeline Home Services. Write it like you're onboarding a new employee: who is Ridgeline, what do they do, who are their customers, how do they communicate, what does a good follow-up message look like. Paste in the sample message Claude generated as a reference. Test it: ask Cowork to draft a follow-up for one overdue lead — paste that lead's row as context. Does it sound like Ridgeline? Is it specific to what the lead inquired about? Iterate until both answers are yes.
  2. 3
    Build the scheduled task. Create a scheduled task in Cowork that runs daily: check the Ridgeline lead sheet, identify any lead whose Follow-Up Status is blank and whose Inquiry Date is more than 48 hours ago, draft a personalized follow-up message for each one using the Ridgeline Skill, and write that draft into the Draft Message column. Connect your Google Sheet via a native Cowork connector or Zapier MCP. Run the task manually first to verify the output before you set it on a schedule. Check the sheet — are the drafts in the right column and do they match your Skill's voice?
  3. 4
    Add one live output for yourself. Wire one additional action via Zapier MCP: when the task completes, send a summary email to your own email address listing how many leads were drafted and the name of each one. This step shows you how Zapier MCP actually behaves in production — what approval prompts look like, what "Always Allow" does, and what happens when it runs autonomously. You're the recipient. Nothing goes anywhere it shouldn't.
Your Deliverable
  • A Cowork Skill for Ridgeline that produces clean, on-brand follow-up drafts
  • A scheduled task that reads the sheet and fills in the Draft Message column
  • A summary email delivered to your own inbox confirming the task ran
  • A Google Sheet with at least three drafted messages
Advanced Extension
Add a second scheduled task that runs Monday mornings and emails you a weekly summary: how many leads were in the sheet, how many were overdue, and a one-line status for each. This is the reporting layer that turns an automation into a system a client can actually trust.
