The Challenge
Real-World Scenario

Module 5 is about building the system that fills your pipeline — research, enrichment, and outreach — running with minimal manual input. This challenge teaches you every step of that system without requiring paid tools, live email sends, or real prospect data. You're building the full pipeline using fictional leads and your own inbox as the only destination. Every tool you touch in this challenge is either free or something you already have. When this is done, you'll understand exactly how a real deployment works — and you'll be able to build it for a client in an afternoon.

Step 1 — Generate your environment

Open Claude and ask it to generate a complete working environment for a fictional boutique real estate investment firm called Summit Properties: an ICP brief describing Summit's ideal client (job title, company size, geography, three problems Summit solves), a prospect list of 15 fictional people matching that ICP with name, title, company, location, and a one-line fit reason, one sample cold outreach email written in Summit's voice, and a Cowork Skill document. Paste the prospect list into a Google Sheet with columns: Name, Title, Company, Location, Fit Reason, Draft Email. Leave Draft Email blank.

Your Challenge
  1. 1
    Build your prospecting playbook. Use Claude's Research mode with the ICP brief as input. Ask it to return: where Summit's ideal investors spend time and congregate online, the language they use to describe their own problems, what signals someone is actively looking for deals right now, and how you'd filter for them on Apollo (free tier) or LinkedIn. This becomes your reference every time you add new leads.
  2. 2
    Build the Outreach Skill and draft the emails. Load your Summit Cowork Skill. Create a task that takes the prospect sheet and writes a personalized cold email for each lead using their title, company, and fit reason as context. Run it against all 15 fictional leads. Read every draft: does it sound like Summit wrote it, or like AI? Refine your Skill until you'd be comfortable putting your name on these. Write the drafted emails into the Draft Email column.
  3. 3
    Stage the campaign. Using Zapier MCP or a Cowork task wired to Gmail, take the five strongest emails and create Gmail drafts — not sends — in your own account. Open Gmail and review them. They should appear exactly as they would before hitting send on a real campaign. This is the staging layer — in a real deployment, you'd review here before pushing to your sending tool.
  4. 4
    Schedule one step. Set up one automated step that runs without you triggering it: every Monday morning, your Cowork task pulls the first five rows from the sheet where Draft Email is blank, writes personalized emails for each, and either fills in the column or drops them into Gmail drafts. Confirm it ran by checking the output the next time it triggers. This is the difference between a task you built and a system you operate.
Your Deliverable
  • A Cowork Skill for Summit that produces emails you'd be willing to send
  • A Google Sheet with 15 drafted emails written by the system, not by you
  • At least five Gmail drafts staged in your own inbox
  • One step running on a schedule autonomously
Extension
Build a call prep Skill for Summit. When you paste a prospect's name and company into a Cowork task, the Skill uses web research to pull their LinkedIn summary, company description, and any recent news — then produces a one-page call prep brief: company overview, likely problems, three questions to open with, and which part of Summit's offer to lead with. Run it against two of your fictional prospects.
Integration note: All outreach in this challenge goes to your own inbox only. Building drafts in Gmail is the professional way to test any outreach system before it touches real contacts — it lets you see the output, catch formatting issues, and verify the personalization actually worked. Every serious operator tests this way.
